Detailed information

Tourist visa

Most people will need a tourist visa to get into Kenya. For US citizens, you can apply online for an e-visa. Or, you can purchase on arrival, but you will need exact cash (USD) to pay for the visa.

The Kenyan immigration service will have the most up-to-date information, including prices and details for non-US citizens.

Money

  • Currently 1 USD = 100 KES
  • Currency exchange apps are available for iOS and Android.
  • Never let your credit card out of sight!
  • ATMs are available and credit cards are widely accepted, but make sure you alert your bank before you leave.

Transport

  • We (Temi and Brian) will arrange for airport pick up.
  • We will hire transport for some excursions, like the mini-safari.
  • For the rest of the transportation you are on your own. We highly recommend you use Uber. It works well in Nairobi, but make sure your billing information is up to date before you leave.

Attire

Kenya is generally conversative in dress. You can get away with shorts, short skirts (short means above the knees here), tank tops at trendy restaurant and bars. But daily life is more conservative--we recommend long pants / skirts / dresses and covered shoulders when walking around town. Lamu, is very conservative. A bikini is acceptable at certain tourist beaches, but you should cover up when walking around town or visiting restaurants.

Health

The CDC website has up-to-date health information about traveling to Kenya.

Mobile Phone

If your phone is unlocked, you can buy a local SIM card. If you are not sure, contact your mobile phone provider before you leave. Safaricom will have the best coverage. Free wifi is available at most cafes in Nairobi.
